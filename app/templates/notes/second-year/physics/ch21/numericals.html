21.1 Find the mass defect and the binding energy for tritium, if the atomic mass of tritium is 3.016049 u.

Given

  1. mass of tritium = m = 3.016049 u
  2. mass of neutron = mn = 1.008665 u
  3. mass of proton = mp = 1.007276 u

To Find

  1. defect = Δm = ?
  2. Binding energy = B.E. = ?

Solution

  1. As, Δm = [Zmp + (A - Z)mn] - m
  2. For tritium, Z = 1 and A = 3. So,
  3. Δm = 1.007276 + 2(1.008665) - 3.01605
  4. Δm = 0.008557 u
  5. Δm = 8.557 × 10-3 u
  6. The binding energy of a nucleus in MeV is,
  7. B.E. = Δm × 931 MeV = 8.557 × 10-3 u × 931 MeV
  8. B.E. = 7.97 MeV

21.2 The half-life of \( _{38}^{91}\textrm{Sr} \) is 9.70 hours. Find its decay constant.

Given

  1. T1/2 = 9.7 hours = 34920 s

To Find

  1. Decay constant = λ = ?

Solution

  1. As,
  2. $$ λ = \frac{ 0.693 }{ T_{1/2} } = \frac{ 0.693 }{ 34920 } $$
  3. $$ λ = 1.98 \times 10^{-5}\ s^{-1} $$

21.3 The element \( _{91}^{234}\textrm{Pa} \) is unstable and decays by β-emission with a half-life 6.66 hours. State the nuclear reaction and the daughter nuclei.

Given

  1. Half life = T1/2 = 6.66 × 60 × 60 = 23976 s

To Find

  1. Nuclear reaction = ?
  2. Daughter nucleus = ?

Solution

  1. As for β-emission the nuclear reaction is:
  2. $$ _{2}^{A}\textrm{X} \longrightarrow\ _{2 + 1}^{A}\textrm{Y} +\ _{-1}^{\ \ \ 0}\textrm{β} $$
  3. $$ _{91}^{234}\textrm{Pa} \longrightarrow\ _{92}^{234}\textrm{X} +\ _{-1}^{\ \ \ 0}\textrm{e} $$
  4. So, \( _{92}^{234}\textrm{X} =\ _{92}^{234}\textrm{U} \)
  5. So the daughter nuclei is Uranium-92.

21.4 Find the energy associated with the following reaction: (mass of \( _{1}^{1}\textrm{H} \) = 1.00784 u)
\( _{7}^{14}\textrm{N} +\ _{2}^{4}\textrm{He} \longrightarrow\ _{8}^{17}\textrm{O} +\ _{1}^{1}\textrm{H} \)
What does the negative sign indicate?

Given

  1. Mass of \( _{7}^{14}\textrm{N} \) = 14.0031 u
  2. Mass of \( _{2}^{4}\textrm{He} \) = 4.00264 u
  3. Mass of \( _{8}^{17}\textrm{O} \) = 16.991 u
  4. Mass of \( _{1}^{1}\textrm{H} \) = 1.0073 u

To Find

  1. Energy associated = Q = ?

Solution

  1. As,
  2. Δm = (mass of \( _{7}^{14}\textrm{N} \) + mass of \( _{2}^{4}\textrm{He} \)) - (mass of \( _{8}^{17}\textrm{O} \) + mass of \( _{1}^{1}\textrm{H} \))
  3. Δm = (14.0031 + 4.002603) - (16.9991 + 1.007825)
  4. Δm = -1.2 × 10-3 u
  5. For energy Q = Δm × 931 MeV
  6. Q = -1.2 × 10-3 u × 931 MeV
  7. Q = -1.2 MeV
  8. The negative sign indicates that this energy is used to start the nuclear reaction.

21.5 Determine the energy associated with the following reaction: (mass of \( _{6}^{14}\textrm{C} \) = 14.0077 u)
\( _{6}^{14}\textrm{C} \longrightarrow\ _{7}^{14}\textrm{N} +\ _{-1}^{\ \ \ 0}\textrm{e} \)

Given

  1. Mass of \( _{6}^{14}\textrm{C} \) = 14.003918 u
  2. Mass of \( _{7}^{14}\textrm{N} \) = 14.0067 u
  3. Mass of \( _{-1}^{\ \ \ 0}\textrm{e} \) = 0.000548 u

To Find

  1. Energy associated = Q = ?

  4. Δm = 0.00167 u
  5. For energy Q = Δm × 931 MeV
  6. Q = 0.00167 × 931
  7. Q = 1.55 MeV

21.6 If \( _{92}^{233}\textrm{U} \) decays twice by α-emission, what is the resulting isotope?

Given

  1. Decaying element = \( _{92}^{233}\textrm{U} \)

To Find

  1. Resulting isotope Z = ?

Solution

  1. For an emission,
  2. $$ _{2}^{A}\textrm{X} \longrightarrow\ _{2-2}^{A-4}\textrm{Y} +\ _{2}^{4}\textrm{He} $$
  3. After first emission,
  4. $$ _{92}^{233}\textrm{U} \longrightarrow\ _{90}^{229}\textrm{X} +\ _{2}^{4}\textrm{He} $$

  6. $$ _{90}^{229}\textrm{X} \longrightarrow\ _{88}^{225}\textrm{Z} +\ _{2}^{4}\textrm{He} $$
  7. Where \( _{88}^{225}\textrm{Z} =\ _{88}^{225}\textrm{Ra} \)

21.8 A sheet of lead 5.0 mm thick reduces the intensity of a beam of γ-rays by a factor 0.4. Find half value thickness of lead sheet which will reduce the intensity to half of its initial value.

Given

  1. Thickness of sheet = x1 = 5 mm = 5 × 10-3 m
  2. Initial intensity of γ ray beam = I1 = 0.4Io
  3. Final intensity of γ ray beam = I2 = 0.5Io

To Find

  1. Thickness of sheet = x2 = ?

Solution

  1. The formula for γ-rays when passing through a material,
  2. $$ I = I_{o}e^{-µx_{1}} $$
  3. So, \( I_{1} = I_{o}e^{-µx_{1}} \)
  4. $$ 0.4I_{o} = I_{o}e^{-µx_{1}} $$
  5. Taking log on both sides,
  6. $$ ln(0.4) = ln(e^{-µx_{1}}) $$
  7. $$ -0.916 = -µx_{1}\ \cdots\cdots\ (i) $$
  8. Similarly,
  9. $$ I_{2} = I_{o}e^{-µx_{2}} $$
  10. Also, \( I_{2} = 0.5I_{o} \), so
  11. $$ 0.5I_{o} = I_{o}e^{-µx_{1}} $$
  12. Taking log,
  13. $$ ln(0.5) = ln(e^{-µx_{2}}) $$
  14. $$ -0.693 = -µx_{2}\ \cdots\cdots\ (ii) $$
  15. Dividing eq. (i) by eq. (ii),
  16. $$ \frac{ 0.916 }{ 0.693 } = \frac{ µx_{1} }{ µx_{2} } $$
  17. $$ x_{2} = \frac{ 0.693 }{ 0.916 } \times 5 \times 10^{-3} $$
  18. $$ x_{2} = 3.79 \times 10^{-3} = 3.79\ mm $$

21.9 Radiation from a point source obeys the inverse square law. If the count rate at a distance of 1.0 m from Geiger counter is 360 counts per minute, what will be its count rate at 3.0 m from the source?

Given

  1. Initial count rate = I1 = 360 count/min
  2. Initial distance of source from G.M. = r1 = 1 m
  3. Final distance of source from G.M. = r2 = 3 m

To Find

  1. Final count rate = I2 = ?

Solution

  1. Since the count rate of G.M. counter is inversely proportional to square of distance from radioactive source,
  2. $$ I \propto \frac{ 1 }{ r^{2} } \implies I = \frac{ k }{ r^{2} } $$
  3. So for r = r1 and r = r2,
  4. $$ I_{1} = \frac{ k }{ r_{1}^{2} }\ \cdots\ (i)\ and\ I_{2} = \frac{ k }{ r_{2}^{2} }\ \cdots\ (ii) $$
  5. Dividing eq. (i) and eq. (ii),
  6. $$ \frac{ I_{1} }{ I_{2} } = \frac{ r_{2}^{2} }{ r_{1}^{2} } \implies I_{2} = I_{1}\left( \frac{ r_{1}^{2} }{ r_{2}^{2} } \right) $$
  7. Then,
  8. $$ I_{2} = 360 \times \frac{ 1 }{ 9 } $$
  9. \( I_{2} = 40 \) count per minute.

21.10 A 75 kg person receives a whole body radiation dose of 24 m-rad, delivered by α-particles for which RBE factor is 12. Calculate (a) the absorbed energy in joules, and (b) the equivalent dose in rem.

Given

  1. mass = m = 75 kg
  2. Absorbed dose = D = 24 m-rad = 24 × 10-3 rad
  3. As 1 rad = 0.01 Gy,
  4. D = 24 × 10-3 × 0.01
  5. D = 24 × 10-5 Gy
  6. RBE factor of radiation = 12

To Find

  1. Energy absorbed in joules = E = ?
  2. Equivalent dose De in rem = ?

Solution

  1. As,
  2. $$ D = \frac{ E }{ m }\ or\ E = D \times m $$
  3. $$ E = 24 \times 10^{-5} \times 75 $$
  4. $$ E = 18 \times 10^{-3} = 18\ mJ $$
  5. Now for De,
  6. $$ D_{e} = D \times RBE = 24 \times 10^{-5} \times 12 $$
  7. $$ D_{e} = 2.9 \times 10^{-3}\ Sv $$
  8. Converting it to rem,
  9. $$ D_{e} = 2.9 \times 10^{-3} \times 100\ rem\ (\because 1\ Sv = 100\ rem) $$
  10. $$ D_{e} = 0.29\ rem $$
