21.1 What are isotopes? What do they have in common and what are their differences?

Isotopes are such nuclei of an element that have same atomic number(Z), but different mass number(A).

Similarities:

  • Isotopes of an element have same number of protons.
  • They have same chemical properties.
Differences:
  • Isotopes of an element have different number of neutrons
  • They have different physical properties

For example, isotopes of Hydrogen are: \( _{1}^{1}\textrm{H} \), \( _{1}^{2}\textrm{H} \) and \( _{1}^{3}\textrm{H} \).

21.2 Why are heavy nuclei unstable?

Heavy nuclei are unstable because of the repulsive electromagnetic forces between protons. Since heavy nuclei, such as Uranium, have a large number of protons and neutrons, these repulsive forces overcome the strong force of attraction which binds together the protons and neutrons in the nucleus, which makes the nuclei unstable.

An unstable nuclei tries to achieve stability by giving off a neutron or proton in a process called radioactive decay.

21.3 If a nucleus has a half-life of 1 year, does this mean that it will be completely decayed after 2 years? Explain.

No, it does not mean that after 2 years it will be completely decayed.

Reason:

If a nucleus has a half-life of 1 year, it means that after 1 year only half of the original number of atoms will remain. So after 2 years, 1/4th(one-fourth) of the original number of atoms (N) will remain.

21.4 What fraction of a radioactive sample decays after two half-lives have elapsed?

3/4 or 75% of the original sample is decayed after two half-lives.

Suppose N is the number of atoms in the radioactive sample. So after one half-life has passed, 1/2 of the original atoms (N) will be decayed. And after two half-lives have passed, 1/4 of the original atoms will be decayed. So the total number of decayed atoms will be:

  1. N/2 + N/4 = N3/4 or 75% of N.

21.6 Describe a brief account of interaction of various types of radiations with matter.

α-particles can ionize and excite atoms of the element through which they pass. The ionization caused by α-particles may be due to direct elastic collision with atoms or due to electrostatic attraction between them and the electrons of the atoms. This property of ionization is used to detect and measure the energy of α-particles. For example, an α-particle creates about 104 ionization in 1 mm of air.

β-particles are fast moving electrons coming out of nuclei of radioactive elements. β-particles are 7000 times lighter than α-particles and their charge is half of the charge on an α-particle. Ionization caused by β-particles is due to electrostatic repulsion between them and electrons of the atom. Ionizing ability of β-particles is about 100 times less than that of α-particles because their mass and charge is smaller than an α-particle's mass and charge. However, their range through gas is about 100 times more than α-particles. Their path is not straight due to deflection caused by collisions with atoms of the medium.

γ-rays are uncharged radiations coming out of the nuclei of radioactive elements. The ionization caused by γ-ray photons is almost negligible. γ-rays interact with matter in three different ways depending on their energy:

  • At low energy, less than 0.5 MeV, γ-rays interact with matter and produce photoelectric effect.
  • At intermediate energy, between 0.5-1.0 MeV, γ-rays produce Compton scattering.
  • At energy higher than 1.02 MeV, the dominant process is pair production.

21.7 Explain how α and β-particles may ionize an atom without directly hitting the electrons? What is the difference in the action of the two particles for producing ionization?

α and β-particles may ionize an atom without directly hitting the electrons as α-particles are positively charged and β-particles are negatively charged. When an α-particle passes by an atom, it attracts its electrons and the atom is ionized. On the other hand, when a β-particle passes by an atom it repels the electrons of the atom and may knock it out, which will ionize the atom.

21.8 A particle which produces more ionization is less penetrating. Why?

The kinetic energy of the particles which produce more ionization is used up quickly in the process, therefore, they have less penetration power. On the other hand, the particles with low ionizing power can travel greater distances in a medium because they have more kinetic energy left.

21.9 What information is revealed by the length and shape of the tracks of an incident particle in Wilson cloud chamber?

Nature and energy of incident particle can be revealed by observing the length and shape of the tracks in Wilson Cloud chamber. For example,
  • The α-particles leave thick, straight and continuous tracks due to intense ionization produced by them.
  • The β-particles form thin and discontinuous tracks in an irregular manner showing frequent deflections.
  • The γ-rays leave no definite tracks along their path. The length of the cloud tracks has been found proportional to the incident particle.

21.10 Why must a Geiger Muller tube for detecting α-particles have a very thin end window? Why does a Geiger Muller tube for detecting γ-rays not need a window at all?

Since α-particle posses less penetrating power than gamma rays, therefore, a thin window allows their entry into Geiger tube easily. For detecting γ-rays, window becomes useless because of their high penetrating power. Therefore, G‐M tube does not need a window for detection of gamma rays.

21.11 Describe the principle of operation of a solid state detector of ionizing radiation in terms of generation and detection of charge carriers.

A solid-state detector is a specially designed p-n junction, operating under a reversed bias in which electron-hole pairs are produced by the incident radiation to cause a current pulse to flow through the external circuit. Then the electrical pulse is amplified and recorded.

21.12 What do we mean by the term critical mass?

The critical mass is defined as the mass of Uranium in which one neutron, out of all the neutrons produced in one fission reaction, produces further fission, or, the minimum mass of a material that can sustain a nuclear chain reaction. It is the quantity of such mass, which is enough to absorb most of neutrons produced in fission chain reaction and to produce a large amount of energy.

21.13 Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of nuclear power compared to the use of fossil fuel generated power.

Advantages:
  • Nuclear power is cheaper than fossil fuel.
  • It does not produce much pollution.
  • Waste products can be reprocessed.
Disadvantages:
  • It has dangerous radiation effect.
  • Its waste products are strongly radioactive, dangerous and are difficult to dump.

21.14 What factors make a fusion reaction difficult to achieve?

When two light nuclei are brought together, a strong force of repulsion comes into play. This force of repulsion prevents the nuclei to fuse together. To overcome this difficulty the nuclei should be moving with very high velocity, which needs temperature of the order 107°C. Such a high temperature is very difficult to produce.

21.15 Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of fission power from the point of safety, pollution and resources.

Advantages:
  • Since the fusion reaction is free from radioactive products, so it will be safe and free from pollution.
  • The fusion reaction produces more energy per nucleon.
  • The energy produced by fusion is cheaper than fission energy.
Disadvantages:
  • It is more difficult to start a fusion reaction.
  • It cannot be controlled like fission reactions.

21.16 What do you understand by "background radiation"? State two sources of this radiation.

G‐M tube records radiation even if there is no radioactive source nearby. This is due to radiation in the environment called background radiation.

Sources:

Background radiation is partly due to cosmic radiation which comes to us from outer space and partly due to naturally occurring radioactive substances in Earth's crust.

21.17 If someone accidentally swallows an α-source and a β-source which would be the more dangerous to him? Explain why?

α-particles are more dangerous than β-particles because they have greater ionizing power as compared to β-particles and γ-rays. So if someone swallows an α-source and a β-source, α-source will be more dangerous as it will cause more damage to the tissues.

21.18 Which radiation dose would deposit more energy to the body
  1. 10 mGy to the hand, or
  2. 1 mGy dose to the entire body.

As we know that:
  • Absorbed dose D = Energy/Mass
  • E = D × Mass
Since the mass of the whole body is far greater than mass of the hand, therefore, the case (b) 1 mGy dose to the entire body deposits more energy to the body.

21.19 What is a radioactive tracer? Describe one application each in medicine, agriculture and industry.

A Radioactive tracer is a radioactive isotope used to trace the path or position of an element through a biological, chemical, or mechanical system.

  • Medicine: In addition to imaging, radio therapy can be used to treat conditions such as hyperthyroidism, thyroid cancer, and blood disorders. For this type of therapy, Yttrium-90 and Iodine-131 are the most commonly used isotopes.
  • Agriculture: Small quantities of labeled nutrients can be accurately measured. It helps to study the effect of one element upon the absorption of another and their interaction by plants. The relationship between the root growth and taking of phosphorous from the soil determines percentage productivity of food grains.
  • Industry: Labelled radioactive Carbon-14, mixed with certain compounds, provides a simple test of leaks in pipes and the flow rates of liquid without effecting the actual flow.

21.20 How can radioactivity help in the treatment of cancer?

γ-rays from Cobalt-60 are often used in the treatment of cancer. The γ-rays are carefully focused on the malignant tissue. Similarly, Iodine-131 is used to combat cancer of the thyroid gland. For skin cancer, Phosphorous-32 or Strontium-90 are used.
