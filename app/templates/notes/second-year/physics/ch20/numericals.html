20.1 A Hydrogen atom is in its ground state (n = 1). Using Bohr's theory, calculate
  1. the radius of the orbit,
  2. the linear momentum of the electron,
  3. the angular momentum of the electron
  4. the kinetic energy
  5. the potential energy, and
  6. the total energy.

Given

  1. n = 1

To Find

  1. r1 = ?
  2. P = ?
  3. L = ?
  4. K.E. = ?
  5. P.E. = ?
  6. Total energy E = ?

Solution

    1. Since \( r_{n} = \frac{ n^{2}h^{2} }{ 4\pi^{2}kme^{2} } \) and n = 1, therefore,
    2. $$ r_{1} = \frac{ (1)^{2} \times (6.63 \times 10^{-34})^{2} }{ 4 \times (3.14)^{2} \times 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \times 9 \times 10^{9} \times (1.6 \times 10^{-19})^{2} } $$
    3. $$ r_{1} = \frac{ 4.395 \times 10^{-67} }{ 8.277 \times 10^{-57} } $$
    4. $$ r_{1} = 0.53 \times 10^{-10}\ m $$
    1. $$ P = mv_{n} = m \times \frac{ 2\pi ke^{2} }{ nh } $$
    2. For n = 1, \( P = \frac{ 2\pi mke^{2} }{ h } \)
    3. $$ P = \frac{ 2 \times 3.14 \times 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \times 9 \times 10^{9} \times (1.6 \times 10^{-19})^2 }{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} } $$
    4. $$ P = 1.99 \times 10^{-24}\ kgms^{-1} $$
    1. $$ L = \frac{ nh }{ 2\pi } $$
    2. $$ L = \frac{ 1 \times 6.63 \times 10^{-34} }{ 2 \times 3.14 } = 1.055 \times 10^{-34} $$
    3. $$ L = 1.055 \times 10^{-34}\ kgm^{2}s^{-1} $$
    1. $$ K.E. = \frac{1}{2}mv_{n}^{2} = \frac{1}{2}\left( \frac{ ke^{2} }{ r_{n} } \right) $$
    2. $$ K.E. = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{ ke^{2} }{ r_{1} } = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{ 9 \times 10^{9} \times (1.6 \times 10^{-19})^{2} }{ 0.53 \times 10^{-10} } $$
    3. $$ K.E. = \frac{ 1.15 \times 10^{-28} }{ 0.53 \times 10^{-10} } = \frac{ 2.17 \times 10^{-18} }{ 1.6 \times 10^{-19} }J $$
    4. $$ K.E. = 13.6\ eV $$
    1. $$ P.E. = -\frac{ ke^{2} }{ r_{n} } $$
    2. $$ P.E. = \frac{ 9 \times 10^{9} \times (1.6 \times 10^{-19})^{2} }{ 0.53 \times 10^{-10} } = -\frac{ 4.35 \times 10^{-18} }{ 1.6 \times 10^{-19} }J $$
    3. $$ P.E. = -27.2\ eV $$
    1. Total energy E = K.E. + P.E.
    2. E = 13.6 + (-27.2) = -13.6
    3. E = -13.6 eV

20.2 What are the energies in eV of quanta of wavelength? λ = 400, 500 and 700 nm.

Given

  1. λ1 = 400 nm
  2. λ2 = 500 nm
  3. λ3 = 700 nm

To Find

  1. E1 = ?
  2. E2 = ?
  3. E3 = ?

Solution

    1. $$ E_{1} = \frac{ hc }{ λ_{1} } = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }{ 400 \times 10^{-9} } $$
    2. $$ E_{1} = 3.10\ eV $$
    1. $$ E_{2} = \frac{ hc }{ λ_{2} } = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }{ 500 \times 10^{-9} } $$
    2. $$ E_{2} = 2.49\ eV $$
    1. $$ E_{3} = \frac{ hc }{ λ_{3} } = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }{ 700 \times 10^{-9} } $$
    2. $$ E_{3} = 1.77\ eV $$

20.3 An electron jumps from a level Ei = -3.5 × 10-19 J to Ef = -1.20 × 10-18 J. What is the wavelength of the emitted light?

Given

  1. Ei = -3.5 × 10-19 J
  2. Ef = -1.2 × 10-18 J

To Find

  1. λ = ?

Solution

  1. As, \( E = hf = E_{f} - E_{i} \)
  2. $$ \frac{ hc }{ λ } = E_{f} - E_{i}\ or\ λ = \frac{ hc }{ E_{f} - E_{i} } $$
  3. $$ λ = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }{ -3.5 \times 10^{-19} - (-1.2 \times 10^{-18}) } $$
  4. $$ λ = -234 \times 10^{-9} $$
  5. $$ λ = 234\ nm\ (\because\ negative\ sign\ ignored\ as\ length\ isn't\ negative) $$

20.4 Find the wavelength of the spectral line corresponding to the transition in Hydrogen from n = 6 state to n = 3 state?

Given

  1. As the transition is from n = 6 to n = 3, so n > p
  2. n = 6
  3. p = 3

To Find

  1. Wavelength = λ = ?

Solution

  1. As,
  2. $$ \frac{1}{λ} = R_{H}\left( \frac{1}{p^{2}} - \frac{1}{n^{2}} \right) $$
  3. $$ \frac{1}{λ} = 1.0974 \times 10^{7}\left( \frac{1}{3^{2}} - \frac{1}{6^{2}} \right) $$
  4. $$ \frac{1}{λ} = 1.0974 \times 10^{7}\left( \frac{1}{9} - \frac{1}{36} \right) $$
  5. $$ \frac{1}{λ} = 1.0974 \times 10^{7}\left( \frac{4 - 1}{36} \right) $$
  6. $$ \frac{1}{λ} = \frac{ 3.29 \times 10^{7} }{ 36 } = 9.145 \times 10^{5} $$
  7. $$ λ = 1.094 \times 10^{-6}\ m $$
  8. $$ λ = 1.094\ nm $$

20.7 Electrons in an X-ray tube are accelerated through a potential difference of 3000 V. If these electrons were slowed down in a target, what will be the minimum wavelength of X-rays produced?

Given

  1. V = 3000 V

To Find

  1. λmin = ?

Solution

  1. $$ K.E._{max} = hf_{max},\ and\ K.E._{max} = Ve $$
  2. $$ Therefore,\ hf_{max} = Ve $$
  3. $$ \frac{ hc }{ λ_{min}} = Ve\ (\because\ for\ f_{max}\ wavelength\ is\ λ_{min}) $$
  4. $$ λ_{min} = \frac{ hc }{ Ve } = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }{ 3000 \times 1.6 \times 10^{-19} } $$
  5. $$ λ_{min} = 4.14 \times 10^{-10}\ m $$

20.8 The wavelength of K X-ray from copper is 1.377 × 10-10 m. What is the energy difference between the two levels from which this transition results?

Given

  1. λ = 1.377 × 10-10 m

To Find

  1. ΔE = ?

Solution

  1. $$ As,\ ΔE = \frac{ hc }{ λ } $$
  2. $$ ΔE = \frac{ 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }{ 1.377 \times 10^{-10} } = 1.44 \times 10^{-15}\ J $$
  3. $$ ΔE = \frac{ 1.44 \times 10^{-15} }{ 1.6 \times 10^{-19} }\ J = 9.03 \times 10^{3}\ eV $$
  4. $$ ΔE = 9.03\ keV $$

20.9 A Tungsten target is struck by electrons that have been accelerated from rest through 40 kV potential difference. Find the shortest wavelength of the bremsstrahlung radiation emitted.

  1. Potential difference = V = 40 kV

To Find

  1. λmin = ?

Solution

  1. $$ Ve = hf_{max} = \frac{ hc }{ λ_{min} } $$
  2. $$ \implies λ_{min} = \frac{ hc }{ Ve } $$
  3. $$ λ_{min} = \frac{ 6.64 \times 10^{-34} \times 3 \times 10^{8} }{ 40000 \times 1.6 \times 10^{-19} } = 3.1 \times 10^{-11}\ m $$
  4. $$ λ_{min} = 0.31 \times 10^{-10}\ m $$

20.10 The orbital electron of a Hydrogen atom moves with a speed of 5.456 × 105 ms-1.
  1. Find the value of the quantum number n associated with this electron.
  2. Calculate the radius of this orbit.
  3. Find the energy of the electrons in this orbit.

Given

  1. Vn = 5.456 × 105 ms-1

To Find

  1. n = ?
  2. rn = ?
  3. En = ?

Solution

    1. $$ As,\ V_{n} = \frac{ 2\pi ke^{2} }{ nh } $$
    2. $$ \implies n = \frac{ 2\pi ke^{2} }{ V_{n}h } $$
    3. $$ n = \frac{ 2 \times 3.14 \times 9 \times 10^{9} \times (1.6 \times 10^{-19})^{2} }{ 5.456 \times 10^{5} \times 6.63 \times 10^{-34} } = 3.999 $$
    4. $$ n = 4 $$
    1. $$ r_{n} = \frac{ n^{2}h^{2} }{ 4\pi^{2}mke^{2} } $$
    2. $$ r_{4} = \frac{ (4)^{2} \times (6.63 \times 10^{-34})^{2} }{ 4 \times (3.14)^{2} \times 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \times 9 \times 10^{9} \times (1.6 \times 10^{-19})^{2} } $$
    3. $$ r_{4} = 0.846 \times 10^{-9}\ m = 0.846\ nm $$
    1. $$ E_{n} = -\frac{ 2\pi^{2}mk^{2}e^{4} }{ n^{2}h^{2} } $$
    2. $$ E_{4} = -\frac{ 2 \times (3.14)^{2} \times 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \times (9 \times 10^{9})^{2} \times (1.6 \times 10^{-19})^{4} }{ 4^{2} \times (6.63 \times 10^{-34})^{2} } $$
    3. $$ E_{4} = -\frac{ 1.36 \times 10^{-19} }{ 1.6 \times 10^{-19} }\ J = -0.8478\ eV $$
    4. $$ E_{4} = -0.85\ eV $$
