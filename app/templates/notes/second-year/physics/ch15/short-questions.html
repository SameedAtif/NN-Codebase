15.1 Does the induced emf in a circuit depend on the resistance of the circuit? Does the induced current depend on the resistance of the circuit?

  1. According to Faraday's law, Ε = -NΔΦ/Δt, so induced emf depends on rate of change of magnetic flux Δ&Phi, and it does not depends on resistance R of the circuit
  2. As I = E/R, so induced current is inversely proportional to resistance R.

15.2 A square loop of wire is moving through a uniform magnetic field. The normal to the loop is oriented parallel to the magnetic field. Is an emf induced in the loop? Give a reason for your answer.

There will be no induced emf produced in the loop, as:
  • Ε = -NΔΦ/Δt
  • here ΔΦ/Δt = 0, as rate of change of flux is zero(normal of loop is parallel to magnetic field B). So,
  • Ε = -NΔΦ/Δt = -N(0) = 0
Therefore, no emf is induced.

15.3 A light metallic ring is released from above into a vertical bar magnet. Viewed from above, does the current flow clockwise or anti-clockwise in the ring?

According to Lenz's law, "Induced current in a conductor is such that, it opposes the cause which produces it". Therefore, when magnetic flux changes in the magnetic ring, an emf is induced and north pole is developed in the ring towards the bar magnet.

As viewed from above, direction of current will be clockwise.

15.4 What is the direction of the current through resistor R in Fig. Q. 15.4, when switch S is
  1. closed
  2. opened

  1. When switch S is closed, magnetic flux changes in second coil and current is induced in it. The current in second coil flows in anti-clockwise direction.
  2. Now, on opening the switch, magnetic flux again changes in second coil and current is induced in it. But this time, current flows in clockwise direction.

15.5 Does the induced emf always act to decrease the magnetic flux through a circuit?

No. According to Lenz's law, if magnetic flux increases then induced emf will act to decrease the magnetic flux. If magnetic flux decreases then the induced emf will act to increase magnetic flux.

Therefore, induced emf does not always acts to decrease magnetic flux.

15.6 When the switch in the circuit is closed, a current is established in the coil and the metal ring jumps upward (Fig. Q:15.6). Why? Describe what would happen to the ring if the battery polarity were reversed?

When switch is closed, the current is setup in the coil. Magnetic flux changes through metallic ring and an induced emf is produced in it.

The face of the ring opposite to coil develops similar poles and experiences repulsion from the sides of coil which makes the ring jump.

If the polarity of the battery is reversed, then the ring will jump upwards again.

15.7 The Fig. Q:15.7 shows a coil of wire in the xy-plane with a magnetic field directed along the y-axis. Around which of the three coordinate axes should the coil be rotated in order to generate an emf and a current in the coil?

An emf and current in the coil is generated when it is rotated along x-axis. No change of flux takes place along y-axis and z-axis, because the coil is parallel to the magnetic field B all the time.

15.8 How would you position a flat loop of wire in a changing magnetic field so that there is no emf induced in the loop?

When the loop is placed in a changing magnetic field in such a way that plane of the loop is parallel to the magnetic field lines, no induced emf will be generated in the loop.

15.9 In a certain region, the Earth's magnetic field points vertically down. When a plane flies due north, which wingtip is positively charged?

As the plane is moving towards north, in a region where magnetic field is vertically down, if all the fingers of right hand are stretched down keeping the thumb erect along the motion of plane (North) then the direction of palm of the right hand will indicate the magnetic force on positive charges towards west. Therefore, positive charges in the body of the plane move towards west wingtip, making it positively charged.

15.10 Show that ε and ΔΦ/Δt have the same units.

The unit of emf (E) is volt, which is given as: $$ V = \frac{ J }{ C }\ \cdots\ (i) $$

Unit of ΔΦ/Δt is webber/s, which can be written as: $$ \frac{ Web }{ s } = \frac{ Tesla.m^{2} }{ s } = \frac{ NA^{-1}m^{-1} . m^{2} }{ s } = \frac{ Nm }{ As } = \frac{ J }{ C } $$ $$ \frac{ Web }{ s } = \frac{ J }{ C }\ \cdots\ (ii) $$ From (i) and (ii), we can say that ε and ΔΦ/Δt have same units.

15.11 When an electric motor, such as an electric drill, is being used, does it also act as a generator? If so, what is the consequence of this?

When a motor like drill machine is working, its armature (coil) is revolving in a uniform magnetic field by a potential difference V. The revolving armature of the motor experiences change in flux, which produces emf, known as back emf of the motor. Hence, a drill machine (or a motor) is also operating like a generator whose generated emf is known as back emf.

15.12 Can a D.C. motor be turned into a D.C. generator? What changes are required to be done?

A D.C. motor can be converted into a D.C. generator. For this, the armature coil of the motor is to be coupled with some rotating body. The rotational motion of the body is transferred to the armature coil of the motor. Due to its rotation, the magnetic flux through the coil changes and an emf is induced at the output. The motor, therefore, becomes a generator.

15.13 Is it possible to change both the area of the loop and the magnetic field passing through the loop and still not have an induced emf in the loop?

Yes. If the plane of the loop is kept parallel to the direction of the magnetic field, no emf will be induced in the loop either by changing its area or by changing the magnetic field.

15.14 Can an electric motor be used to drive an electric generator with the output from the generator being used to operate the motor?

No, it's not possible. Because if it is possible, it will be a self-operating system without getting energy from some external source, which is against the law of conservation of energy.

15.15 A suspended magnet is oscillating freely in a horizontal plane. The oscillations are strongly damped when a metal plate is placed under the magnet. Explain why this occurs?

Due to the oscillations of the magnet, the magnetic flux passing through the metallic plate changes. This produces an induced emf in the plate and hence an induced current. The induced current produces its own magnetic field which always opposes the motion of the bar magnet. Therefore, the oscillatory motion of the bar magnet is damped.

15.17
  1. Can a step-up transformer increase the power level?
  2. In a transformer, there is no transfer of charge from the primary to the secondary. How is, then, the power transferred?

  1. No. A step-up transformer can only increase the voltage level at its secondary. It cannot increase the power level in the light of law of conservation of energy.

15.18 When the primary of a transformer is connected to A.C. mains, the current in it:
  1. is very small if the secondary circuit is open, but
  2. increases when the secondary circuit is closed.
Explain these facts.

  1. If the secondary output is open, the output power (VsIs) will be zero. Since output power of a transformer is always slightly smaller than the input power, so a very small current is drawn by the transformer from the A.C. mains, i.e. the input current (Ip) is very small.
  2. When the secondary circuit is closed, the output power increases. To produce this power, transformer will draw large current from the A.C. mains to increase its primary power (VpIp).
