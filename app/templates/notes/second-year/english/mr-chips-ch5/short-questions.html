1. What was Katherine's first impression about Mr. Chips?

When Katherine first met Chips, she thought that he was a solicitor or a stockbroker or a dentist or a man with a big cotton business in Manchester. Moreover, she also considered him a quiet, serious-looking and middle-aged man.

2. What were Mr. Chips' memories about the Lake District after his retirement?

Although Mr. Chips had never visited Lake District since 1896 (after his first meeting with Katherine), he could still remember many things. He could clearly recall the glorious hump of Gable, mouse grey depths of Wastwater lake and the long and narrow path of the pass.

3. How did Mr. Chips felt before his marriage?

Both Mr. Chips and Katherine were very eager to plan their marital life. Chips, however, was very serious, rather a little awed. He was worried about her adjustment in Brookfield and her expectations from him.

4. What did Katherine think of Chips' profession?

Katherine thought that school mastering was a very different and important profession. According to her, a school master influences those who are going to matter to the world. Chips, however, did not think of it like that.

5. How did Mr. Chips depreciate himself before Katherine, before his marriage?

Mr. Chips depreciated himself before Katherine, by telling her about his mediocre degree, his difficulties in discipline, the certainty of no promotion, and his ineligibility to marry a young and ambitious girl. At the end of it all, Katherine laughed in answer.

6. How did Katherine feel about her marriage with Mr. Chips?

On the night before the wedding, Katherine said that she felt like a new boy beginning his first term with him. She asked him if she should call him 'sir' or 'Mr. Chips'. In the end, she opted to call him 'Mr. Chips'.

7. Why did Chips sometimes look at his feet when he lived at Mrs. Wickett's?

When Chips, dreaming through the hours at Mrs. Wickett's, recollected those days, he used to look down at his feet and wondered which one of the feet was that which rendered him such a great service.
