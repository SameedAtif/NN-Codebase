1. When was Mr. Chips appointed housemaster?

Mr. Chips was appointed housemaster in the spring of 1896.

2. Who was Rowdon? Why did he leave Chips alone at the Lake District?

Rowdon was a colleague of Chips. He went with Mr. Chips to the Lake District during the summer vacation of 1896.

3. Which event in the novel led to a great change in Mr. Chips' life?

The most interesting event in the life of Mr. Chips was his first encounter with his wife, Katherine Bridges, at the Great Gable. This event was too sudden in its happening and too surprising, in its outcome, to believe. However, it happened and Katherine entered his life out of the blue to cause great changes.

4. How did Chips came across Katherine Bridges?

In the spring of 1896, Chips came across Katherine Bridges quite suddenly and unexpectedly.

Climbing on the Great Gable, he noticed a girl waving excitedly, signaling to her friend, at a dangerous looking ledge. He hastened to help her but wrenched his ankle in doing so. The girl thought herself responsible for this incident and came down to help Mr. Chips. That girl was Katherine Bridges.

5. What were Chips' ideas about women?

Mr. Chips did not like or care for women. He never felt at ease with them. Especially, the new women of the nineties filled him with horror. He thought of the modern women as a monstrous creature.

However, he presumed that nice women were weak, timid and delicate. And that nice men treat them with distant chivalry.

6. What kind of person was Mr. Chips, and what were his views about the world?

Mr. Chips was a quiet, conventional person. The world, viewed from the shelter of Brookfield, seemed to him full of distasteful innovations. He did not like modernization and freedom. He disliked the opinions of Bernard Shaw, plays of Ibsen and the concept of women bicycling.

7. What did Mr. Chips think of Bernard Shaw and Ibsen?

Mr. Chips disliked Shaw and Ibsen because they were radical writers, whereas, Mr. Chips was a conventional person. According to him, the opinions of Bernard Shaw were the strangest and the most objectionable. He also thought of Ibsen's plays as disturbing.

8. How did Katherine Bridges look? What was her profession?

Katherine Bridges was twenty five years old. She had a sweet and attractive personality. She had smooth straw coloured hair, blue flashing eyes, and freckled cheeks.

She was a governess out of job: She rendered her services as a teacher and care-taker.

9. What were the political views of Katherine Bridges?

Katherine Bridges read and admired radical writers like Ibsen, Bernard Shaw and William Morris. She believed that women ought to be admitted to the universities. She even thought that they had a right to vote.

10. What did Mr. Chips think of Katherine Bridges at first?

Mr. Chips thought of Katherine Bridges as a typical modern woman that filled him with horror. He could not imagine a woman riding a bicycle and visiting a man alone. Mr. Chips did not know what he could do to such a person.

11. What was attractive about Katherine to Mr. Chips?

Katherine captivated Chips with her charming manners and straightforwardness. Her aggressive personality dominated all his vague ideas about women. He had never met anyone like her. He had to admit that she was a beautiful girl.

12. How did Chips respond to the opinions of Katherine?

Mr. Chips absorbed whatever Katherine shared with him on different issues, even if he disagreed with her. In fact, he did not contradict her at all, because he was not very expressive.

13. What was it that Katherine liked about Mr. Chips?

Katherine liked Mr. Chips because he had gentle and quiet manners, he was so hard to get to know, his opinion, although outdated, were very honest. And he looked charming when he smiled.

14. When and where were Chips and Katherine married?

Within a week of their dramatic meeting, they were head over heels in love with each other. They were married in London, a week before the beginning of the autumn term.

15. How was Chips brought home from the Great Gable?

When Chips hastened to rescue Katherine, he slipped and wrenched his ankle. After this, he could barely walk, and it was a hard job getting him down the steep track to Wasdale Head, but Katherine and her friend did this job bravely.

16. Why did Katherine visit Chips on her bicycle?

Katherine used to visit Chips, because she considered herself responsible for his accident. She used to bicycle along the side of the lake to the house where Mr. Chips was staying.

17. Did Katherine stay on the farmhouse when her friend went away?

Yes. Katherine stayed on the farmhouse when her friend went away. Considering herself responsible for Chips' sprain, she continued her stay on the farmhouse to look after him.

18. Where did Chips go and sit when he could limp with sticks after the accident?

Chips used to hobble with sticks along a footpath leading to the tiny church. He used to sit there on a stone slab on the wall. It was comfortable for him to sit down facing the sunlight and the green-brown majesty of the Gable.

19. What were Katherine's views about middle-aged bores?

Katherine had always thought that middle-aged who read the Times and disapproved modernity were terrible bores.

20. What did Katherine say to Mr. Chips when she learnt that his nickname was 'Chips' at school?

Katherine said that she would also call him 'Chips', when she learned that his nickname was 'Chips' at school.
