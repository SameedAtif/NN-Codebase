1. How did Katherine adjust herself in Brookfield?

Katherine was received quite cordially. She didn't have any difficulty in adjusting herself there. She conquered Brookfield as she had conquered Mr. Chips. She was popular among boys and masters alike. Even the wives of the masters could not resist her charms.

2. How did Mr. Chips' marriage prove?

Chips' marriage with Katherine was a success. He still could not believe that anyone could have enjoyed such a marvellous time before or since his marriage.

3. What sort of person was Mr. Chips before his marriage? What was his status in Brookfield?

Mr. Chips had been a dry rather neutral sort of person before his marriage. He was liked and thought well of by Brookfield. He, however, did not get much love and affection from people.

Having served Brookfield for over a quarter of century, he could only make people believe that he was a decent and very capable fellow.

4. Describe Mr. Chips as a teacher before his marriage.

As a teacher, Mr. Chips had his problems. His discipline was not reliable. He was simply following a routine. There was no freshness and innovation. Although he was a hard worker, yet he was a fixture. He was giving service, satisfaction, confidence and everything except inspiration.

5. What is the pitfall in the teaching profession?

The pitfall in the teaching profession is that a teacher ultimately begins to sink into the creeping dry-rot of pedagogy. Giving the same lessons year after year develops a routine for him in which the other affairs of his life adjust themselves.

6. How did Katherine change Mr. Chips?

Katherine changed Mr. Chips by helping him identify and exploit his old, unguessed, and imprisoned potentials. She made him a new man to all appearance. As a result, Mr. Chips excelled both as a person and a professional.

His eyes gained sparkle and his sense of humour became rich. He started to make functional jokes in his class. He became less rigid and more popular. Most important of all, his students started loving him.

7. How did Chips' jokes change and how did it effect the students?

Chips' humour blossomed into a sudden richness. He began to make little jokes. Most of his jokes were mnemonics and puns. These jokes not only raised laughs but also imprinted something on the students' minds.

8. What is Lex Canuleia?

It is a Latin phrase, the name of an ancient law of the Roman Republic, passed in the year 445 BC, restoring the right of marriage between patricians (upper class) and plebeians (lower class).

9. How did Katherine broaden Chips' views and opinions?

Katherine broadened Chips' views and opinions by giving him an outlook broader than the Brookfield. He began to see his country as something deep and gracious to which Brookfield was one of many feeding streams. Her young idealism worked upon his maturity and made him a gentle and wise person.

10. Why could Mr. Chips not confute Katherine's ideas?

Katherine had a clever brain. And Mr. Chips was not an articulate person. Therefore, he would just absorb whatever she said and did not confute her even if he disagreed.

11. Why did Katherine suggest conducting a soccer match between Brookfield and a team from the mission?

Katherine suggested conducting a soccer match between Brookfield and a team from mission because of her moral and social beliefs. She disliked class distinction and high handed generosity. She did not appreciate people giving contributions generously to mission without considering those people alike.

12. How revolutionary was Katherine's idea of a soccer match between Brookfield and the mission?

The idea was so revolutionary that everyone in Brookfield opposed it. All the staff was against it. They thought of Katherine as a reckless person who was trying to disturb things.

It seemed unreasonable as everyone thought that the slum boys of the mission school were no match for the rich boys of Brookfield. It was believed that the slum boys would be hooligans and that there would be incidents.

13. How did Katherine convince Chips for a soccer match between Brookfield and the mission school?

Katherine convinced Chips for a soccer match between Brookfield and the mission school by enlightening him about the importance of the match. She told him that those boys were as important to England as the Brookfieldians. She told Chips that England wasn't always going to be divided into officers and 'other ranks'.

Finally, Chips gave way. He suddenly became a keen advocate of the proposal. Even the authorities consented to the dangerous experiment.

14. How did the experiment of a soccer match between the mission school and Brookfield prove?

Surprisingly, the dangerous experiment was a great success. The boys from Poplar arrived at Brookfield, played soccer with the School's second team. They were honourably defeated by seven goals to five. Later, they also had high tea with the Brookfield team. They took away with them as fine an impression as they had left behind.

15. What were the three things Chips wanted to get when he joined Brookfield?

Chips aimed to be loved, honoured and obeyed, when he joined Brookfield. At the end of his career, he got all these three things.

16. Who came to see Chips during the war, at a military camp stationed near Brookfield?

One of the players of Poplar boys' team came to meet Mr. Chips. He was a soldier and very fond of meeting Katherine. Chips told him about her death, entertained him with tea, and both shared her memories. The soldier told that they were very impressed by her.

The soldier who came to visit Chips, was killed during the war in Passchendaele.

17. How did Katherine help Chips in the matter of discipline?

Chips' discipline, as a teacher, was very weak. His approach was very rigid. Katherine advised him not to punish the students all the time. She advised him to be kind with the students. She motivated him to gain respect and love.
