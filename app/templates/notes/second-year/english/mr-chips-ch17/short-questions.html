1. What things Chips had not done all his life?

Chips had not travelled by air. He had also never been to a talk show. In this way, he was both more and less experienced than the youngest boy at the school.

2. Where had Mrs. Wickett gone a day before Mr. Chips died?

Mrs. Wickett had gone out to visit her relatives in a neighbouring village. She had left the tea things ready on the table, with bread and butter and extra cups in case any body called.

3. Who was the last visitor of Mr. Chips?

Linford, a small boy, came to see Mr. Chips in his last days. In fact, he was the last student who visited him. He was new and the first of his family at Brookfield. He was feeling a little awed at Brookfield.

4. Why did Linford came to visit Mr. Chips?

The boys sent Linford to meet Mr. Chips as a joke. The boys told him that Mr. Chips wanted to see him. In fact, sending every new boy at Brookfield to Mr. Chips in the same way was an old leg pull. Chips did not mind the joke, rather, he was amused by it.

5. Why had Chips not seen Linford before?

The boy, Linford, had actually been in the sanatorium due to measles since the begining of term, and had only just come out of it. That's why Chips had not seen him before.

6. How did Chips reassure Linford, the small boy, as he found him a bit afraid of Brookfield?

As Chips found Linford a bit afraid, he said that he would like Brookfield when he will get used to it. Chips reassured him by telling that he himself was scared when he first went into the Big Hall. He said that he had never been so scared in his life.

7. What were the feelings of Chips when Linford had gone away? OR What did Linford say to Mr. Chips as the latter shook hands and said good bye to him?

Linford said, "Good-bye Mr. Chips," as he left. His words echoed in Chips' mind. He remembered that Katherine had used the same phrase on the eve of their wedding day. Suddenly, tears began to roll down his cheeks. He felt very tired, but he was glad he had met Linford.

8. How did Chips recieve Linford?

Chips was not expecting any visitor on a foggy day, but he received Linford in his usual warm way. Chips knew that the old boys had sent Linford as a leg-pull, but he entertained the boy warmly.
