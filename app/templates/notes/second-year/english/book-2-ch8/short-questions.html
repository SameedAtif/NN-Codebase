1. Why has the world changed its attitude towards China?

The world has changed its attitude towards China because of the remarkable change and progress it has showed in different fields. The exemplary social experiment and superb agricultural and industrial advancement have made China a fashion around the world.

2. Discuss Chinese agriculture system?

Agriculture is thriving in China through the system of communes. These communes are further subdivided into production brigades and teams. The farmers carry on their traditional, intensive labour. They do not migrate to cities but are kept on the spot and incorporated in local small industries.

3. How does China rely on its own resources?

The Chinese follow Mao's famous maxim, "Rely on your own resources." They believe that local equipment can produce bigger, faster, better and cheaper results. It can also create the revolutionary spirit of the masses to overcome the difficulties.

4. Describe a day in the life of a Chinese student?

A Chinese student gets up at 6 A.M., does a few household jobs, and goes to school at 8. At 11 o'clock he/she goes home and has lunch, and at 1:30 he/she returns to school again until 3 o'clock. From 4 o'clock on he/she relaxes reading the papers and listening to the news.

5. Write a note on the Chinese women?

From a Western point of view, there is a lack of femininity in the Chinese women. Chinese women are working shoulder to shoulder with the men. They enjoy many social security benefits, e.g. eight hours working a day, 56 paid leaves for child birth etc. They are striving to achieve these benefits for all the women of the world.

6. What are the social security benefits provided to the Chinese workers?

Each factory or agricultural commune has a health centre, a cafeteria and a nursery and kindergarten. All medical and hospital treatment is free for every Chinese worker. He recieves his total pay for the first six months, and after that 60 percent of his salary.

7. "It is the people and not the things that are decisive." Discuss.

When Mao says that it is the people and not the things that are decisive, he wants to demonstrate that the real power is the people of a country, and not the things or machines. It means that the power of people cannot be crushed by modern technology.

8. "The heart of the matter is the need to root out selfishness." Discuss.

The writer says the most important thing is to root out selfishness and bring into existence selfless and dedicated men whose happiness lies in serving their fellow-men. This has been the guiding principle of exemplary Chinese social experiment. China has now become a powerful nation owing to the selflessness and dedication of its people.
