1. Why had Mr. Chips never liked Ralston?

Mr. Chips had never liked Ralston because of his strictness. Although he was efficient, energetic and ambitious, yet ruthless and not likeable. Mr. Chips also did not like the way Ralston was trying to run Brookfield.

2. What good Ralston did to Brookfield?

Ralston had admittedly raised the status of Brookfield as a school. For the first time in history, there was a longish waiting list of students. He also doubled the school endowment funds.

3. What was Mr. Chips' attitude towards Ralston?

Mr. Chips never bothered to beware of Ralston although he knew that Ralston did not like him. He served him quite loyally and willingly. Actually, he was serving Brookfield more than serving Ralston.

4. Why did Chips never bother to beware of Ralston?

Chips had never bothered to beware of Ralston because he felt himself sufficiently protected in his job by his age and seniority. He knew that his age and seniority would prevent him from suffering the fate of masters whom Ralston did not like.

5. Why did Mr. Chips quarrel with Ralston, the new headmaster?

Mr. Chips quarrelled with Ralston because Ralston insistently suggested Mr. Chips to get retired. He further said that he would be sufficiently pensioned if he thought about the retirement. Mr. Chips felt startled at the suggestion and wondered why Ralston could have suggested so. The suggestion was so sudden and annoying for Mr. Chips that he flared up and the quarrel started.

6. What charges did Ralston put on Mr. Chips?

Ralston levelled the following charges on Mr. Chips:
  1. He had not been pulling his weight around in Brookfield for sometime.
  2. His method of teaching were slack and old-fashioned.
  3. His personal habits were slovenly.
  4. He ignored Ralston's instructions.

7. Why did Ralston not like Mr. Chips?

Ralston did not like Mr. Chips because, according to him, Mr. Chips had not been pulling his weight around in Brookfield for sometime. His methods of teaching were slack and old-fashioned. His personal habits were slovenly. He ignored Ralston's instructions.

8. Why did Ralston accuse Mr. Chips of having slovenly habits?

Mr. Chips used to wear a tattered gown without any regrets. His gown was also a subject of continual amusement throughout the school. That's why, Ralston accused Mr. Chips of having slovenly habits.

9. Did Ralston really think that Chips did his insubordination?

No. In fact, Ralston said that in a younger man he should have regarded Chips' ignoring his instructions as insubordination. Every other teacher was following the new style, however, in Chips' case it was probably a mixture of laziness and stubbornness.

10. Why Mr. Chips did not adopt the new Latin pronunciation?

Chips did not adopt the new Latin pronunciation because he thought it was a nonsense. He said it was foolish to make boys say "Kicker" at school when for the rest of their lives they would say "Cicero". Moreover, it was ridiculous to make boys say "We kiss 'im" instead of "vicissim".

11. According to Mr. Chips how was Ralston trying to run Brookfield?

According to Mr. Chips, Ralston was trying to run Brookfield like a factory that produced snob culture based on money and machines. Instead of taking students from every social class, he was taking only the rich students in Brookfield. Most importantly, rather than cultivating sense of proportion in them he was more focused on examination, certificates and so-called efficiency and up-to-dateness.

12. How did Ralston bring wealthy students to Brookfield?

Ralston met wealthy fellows in London clubs and persuaded them that Brookfield was a coming school. Since they could not buy their way into Eton or Harrow, they greedily swallowed the bait and sent their sons to Brookfield.

13. According to Chips what ought Brookfield to teach the students?

According to Mr. Chips, the most important thing that Brookfield ought to teach was the 'sense of proportion'. It's more important than Latin, Greek, Mathematics and Mechanics.

14. How did students, teachers and parents respond to the row of Mr. Chips with Ralston?

There was amazing outburst of sympathy for Chips after everyone came to know about the row between Mr. Chips and Ralston. Chips had never imagined such a support and sympathy. There was even a talk of public riot in the school if Ralston succeeded in banishing Chips.

15. How did the students and their parents come to know about the row between Chips and Ralston?

When Chips and Ralston were having the argument a small boy overheard the argument. He was outside the Ralston's study to see him. He told his friends about it and they told the story to their parents. Very soon, it was common knowledge that Ralston had insulted Chips and had demanded his resignation.

16. Why did everyone support Mr. Chips after his row with Ralston?

Everyone supported Mr. Chips after his row with Ralston because no one liked Ralston. He was thoroughly unpopular. Moreover, Mr. Chips was more than a teacher for them. He was the defender of Brookfieldian tradition. Brookfield would never be the same without him.

17. What was the reaction of people towards Ralston after his quarrel with Ralston?

After his quarrel with Ralston, the reaction of people towards Ralston was quite astonishing. It turned out that Ralston was thoroughly unpopular. He was feared and respected but not liked. In the issue of Chips, the dislike rose to a point where it conquered fear and demolished even respect. The masters rallied round Chips because they hated Ralston's slave driving. Moreover, they saw in Chips a likely champion.

18. What did Chairman of the Governors tell Chips about his retirement?

When John Rivers visited Brookfield, he ignored Ralston and went direct to Chips. He told Chips that he was sorry to hear about the row. He said that the governors were with him and they did not want him to resign because Brookfield would not be the same without him.

19. What sort of person was Ralston? OR What were the qualities of Ralston?

Ralston was efficient, ruthless and ambitious. He was glittered with First and Blues. He was very pontifical and could reduce Big Hall to silence by the mere lifting of an eyebrow. He had raised the status of Brookfield as a school. His thoughts was modern, therefore, he disliked Chips' conservatism. He was a live wire and a fine power transmitter.

20. What did Ralston say when Chips said that he would not change his Latin pronunciation?

When Chips said that he would not change his Latin pronunciation, Ralston replied that both of them held different approaches. He further said that he wanted to make Brookfield thoroughly modern while Chips was unwilling to change his style and pronunciation. According to Ralston, Chips was teaching dead languages with a dead educational technique.

21. What did Chips say when Ralston said that Chips' teaching method was the same as ten years ago when he joined Brookfield?

Chips defended his method of teaching by giving the reference of Wetherby and Meldrum. Chips stated that his methods were same when Meldrum became the headmaster of Brookfield, thirty eight years ago. He further said that Wetherby also approved his syllabus and methods of teaching.

22. What did Ralston say when Chips said that his method of teaching was approved by Meldrum and Wetherby?

When Chips said that his method of teaching was approved by Meldrum and Wetherby, Ralston said that once again it proved his point of view. He further said that Chips lived too much in the past, and not enough in the present and further. Time was changing whether chips realized it or not. Modern parents were beginning to demand something more than a few scraps of dead languages.

23. What was it according to Chips which Ralston was distorting by bringing rich boys into Brookfield?

According to Chips, Ralston was distorting the sense of proportion by bringing rich boys into Brookfield. Instead of giving preference to Latin, Greek, Chemistry or Mechanics, sense of proportion should be taught in Brookfield as stated by Chips.

24. What did Chips say to Ralston at the end of their row when he was leaving the room?

When Ralston accused Chips a lot about his teaching style and conservative personality, Chips didn't show any willingness to the former. When he was leaving the room of the headmaster, Chips said that he didn't want to resign and Ralston might do whatever he liked.

25. How did Chips feel for Ralston, when he remembered their row, years after his retirement?

Chips felt a little sorry for Ralston, when he remembered their row, in the calm perspective after a quarter of a century. Particularly as it happened, Ralston had been in complete ignorance of the forces he was dealing with.

26. What did the Chairman of the Governors tell Chips about Ralston?

When Sir John Rivers, the Chairman of the Governors, visited Brookfield after the clash had taken place between Chips and Ralston, he ignored the Head and went direct to Chips. He told him that Governors didn't like Ralston a great deal. He further stated that Ralston was very clever. He said that a chap like Ralston wanted watching.

27. What did Chips tell Mrs. Wickett about Rivers (The Chairman of Governors)?

Mr. Chips told dozen times to Mrs. Wickett that John Rivers was a fine fellow. He further said that Rivers was a very brilliant boy in class and he could never master his verbs. Furthermore, he said that they had made Rivers a baronet in his practical life.
