1. According to the author there are some boys who fail because they do no try. Who are they? Can we help them?

The boys who fail because they do not try suffer from nervous habits. They cannot concentrate on the tasks assigned, although they spend more time in the presence of an open book than many a boy of equal ability.

As for help, only they can help themselves. They must take themselves by collar to cultivate poise and calm in their personalities.

2. How does mistaken ambition on the part of boys and their parents lead to the failure of boys?

Mistaken ambition is the distasteful direction for a student mapped by his parents. A victim of mistaken ambition fails because he cannot convince himself to do a work that runs counter to all his interests and abilities. Since he does not take any interest in his work, he fails.

3. There are some boys who have done well at school but fail to make their mark at college. Who are they?
OR
Which boy is the most pitiable object among all our failing students?

The bright boy who has done his work without effort is the most pitiable object among all the failing students. He supposes that he can float through the college with as little effort as he did through school. He depends too much on his ability to get things done quickly.

4. How does financial pressure lead to the failure of students described in the lesson?

Sometimes financial pressure leads to the failure of students. There are some students who earn and learn at the same time. As the writer says, "earning with learning is a difficult process." They get not only a very poor living by the process, but a very poor education as well. Their overwork leads them to failure.

5. To what extent does the question of health lead to the failure at college? How far can the college authorities with their medical officers help students in such cases?

The question of health is always one of the major reasons for failure at college. A surprising large number of students suffer from different diseases unknowingly and fail. If a good health service is available in the college, and if proper cooperation exists between the teaching staff and the college doctor, a huge number of failures can be avoided.

6. What places would you accord to sportsmen in colleges?

If a boy is too much interested in sports and not in his studies, he ought to get out of the main tent and become professional. However, if he could maintain a balance between his studies and sports, he does have a respectable place in college.

7. There are some students who join college for the fun of it. Should they be allowed to stay?

A few lazy bluffers enter into college, but they do not have any serious interest in life. It is usually wise to let them find out by experience how much demand is there for the bluffers in practical life.
