1. When and why did Ralston leave Brookfield School?

Ralston left Brookfield in 1911. He left Brookfield to "better himself". He was offered the headship of one of greater public schools. Moreover, after his quarrel with Mr. Chips the circumstances had not been favourable for him there.

2. Who was the successor of Ralston and what kind of man was he?

Chatteris was the successor to Ralston. Chips liked Chatteris. He was only thirty four, even younger than Ralston. He was modern, friendly and sympathetic. He acknowledged Chips' services to Brookfield institution and very courteously accepted the situation.

3. Why did Mr. Chips decided to resign in 1913?

Mr. Chips decided to resign in 1913 because of bronchitis. He was off duty for the whole of the winter term. He felt that it would be unfair to hang on if he could not do his job decently.

4. How did Mr. Chips convince himself to get retired?

After Mr. Chips had been off duty for the whole winter because of bronchitis, he thought of getting retired. He thought that it was unfair to hang on if he couldn't do his job decently. He convinced himself by telling that he couldn't sever his bond with Brookfield completely.

5. Describe the farewell of Mr. Chips.

Mr. Chips' farewell speech was not very long but it was prolonged with laughter which checked its progress. There were several Latin quotations and a reference to the captain of the school.

6. What did Chips say about the captain of the school in his farewell speech?

Chips said that the captain of the school had been guilty of exaggeration in speaking of his (Chips') services to Brookfield. Chips added that the captain came of an exaggerating family as he (Chips) once had to punish his father for exaggerating one mark for a Latin translation into a seven.

7. What changes and incidents did Chips talk about in his farewell?

Chips said that he remembered the first bicycle, and the time when there was no gas or electric light. He told the audience how the whole school learned to skate on fens when there was a hard frost, and how in 1880's Big Hall was turned into a hospital ward when German measles broke out.

8. What was it that Chips said he chiefly remembered in his farewell speech?

Chips said that he chiefly remembered his students' faces. He said he had thousands of faces in his minds, and his students never grew up at all. He asked his students to think of him sometimes as he would certainly think of them.

9. How did Chips still remain a part of Brookfield even after his retirement?

Chips did not sever himself completely from Brookfield after his retirement. He lived just across the road from Brookfield at Mrs. Wickett's. He had all the new boys to tea, watched the matches on Brookfield ground, dined with the Head as well as the masters, once a term, and edited the edition of Brookfield directory.

10. How did Ralston and Chips live together for three years after the row?

Ralston and Chips did their respective jobs in Brookfield. Chips willingly served Brookfield not Ralston. Chips was also favoured by Chairman of the Governors, John Rivers, who told Chips that the governors were with him and they wanted him to stay in Brookfield. On the other hand, Ralston left Brookfield in 1911, when he was offered a headship of a greater school.

11. How much time did Chips spend in Brookfield?

Chips joined Brookfield in 1870 during Mr. Wetherby's time. He continued serving Brookfield till 1913, when he got retirement because of bronchitis. This means he spent forty-two (42) years in Brookfield.

12. Who was Mrs. Brools? Why did she leave for Australia?

During the farewell Chips talked about Mrs. Brools, who worked in the tuck-shop of Brookfield. Her photograph was still there in the tuck-shop. She served there until an uncle in Australia left her a lot of money.

13. How did Chips describe the Chairman of Governors?

Chips in his farewell speech, pointed out in a comic mood that the respected chairman of Governors was a jolly little chap with hair that stick up on top. He also stated that John Rivers could not have any idea about the difference between Gerund and a Gerundive in his school days.

14. Did Chips read any newspaper?

Chips remained quite busy and quite happy after retirement. One of his occupations was to read the newspaper "Times" every morning thoroughly. Since the first thrills of Sherlock, he had been very keen on reading newspaper and detective stories.

15. What was the event of bonfire in Brookfield?

Chips in his farewell speech, talked about the great bonfire they had on Mafeking night. It was lit too near the pavilion and they had to send for the fire brigade to put it out.
