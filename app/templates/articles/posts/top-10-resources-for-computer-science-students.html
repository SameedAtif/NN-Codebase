Here we present the best of the best online free resources you can use to increase your understanding of Computer Science and the art of programming, so you can stay ahead from the rest of the class.

1. Getting Comfortable

First of all, if you are not even comfortable with computers, it will be impossible for you to get along with coding, let alone be good at it. I’ve even seen some students who are scared of using MS-Word and MS-Excel as they don't have slightest clue on what to do with it and how to use it.

For that, I suggest you start with HTML (Hyper Text Markup Language). Though, not a programming language, it will definitely make you comfortable with writing code. And if you have time and willpower, learn CSS (Cascading Style Sheets) along with HTML. That way, you will be able to make more than basic webpages.

W3Schools and Codecademy are some of the best places to get started with HTML and CSS.

2. CS50: Introduction To Computer Science

Now is the time to decide whether Computer Science is for you or not. Harvard’s Introduction To Computer Science And The Arts Of Programming, will help you decide that.

Seriously, this is the best thing on the internet to introduce you to computer science. Taught by Professor David J. Malan, CS50 will teach you The Art of Computer Programming and Computer Science. Using C language, you will learn:

  • the basic constructs of a computer program
  • pointers
  • data structures
  • the art of problem solving
Scary stuff at first glance(I know because I have been there), but don't worry it has a vast community that will be with you every step of the way and will help you through the tough times. Later on in the course you will learn about the web and how to make web applications. CS50 used PHP for this part of the course, but now it uses Python which makes it even more exciting.

It is available on Edx for free (You can also pay for a verified certificate). You can work on it at your own pace. If you stick to it, you will be able to complete it in less than 5 months. Once you complete it, you will do great in your academic career and beyond (Believe me, you will learn more from this than your entire time at university).

3. CodinGame & Hackerrank

Step up you coding skills by practicing on these new innovative platforms. These platforms are better explained in their own words, “The new way to improve your programming skills while having fun and getting noticed.”

CodinGame allows you to write the logic for games their experts have created. Along the way you learn new concepts while solving fun challenges. What’s more, you can also participate in worldwide tournaments from your home. You can win prizes and also get a well-paying job if you perform good in these competitions.

Hackerrank provides simple to complex problems for you to solve in the language of your choice.You can participate in competitions, and even create your own competitions. You can find good jobs here, too.

You can use these websites to brush up your own skills and to learn more

4. Networking Fundamentals

It is quite essential to learn how the Internet (“computer networks”, to be more specific) work. Microsoft’s Networking Fundamentals is the best short course available online which will explain everything you need to know, including LAN, WAN, Internet and the different protocols you use daily without ever knowing about them.

Now that I have mentioned this course, I would also like to say that Microsoft Virtual Academy is a great resource for learning different technical topics, and Microsoft technologies specifically.

5. Design Patterns

If you have completed CS50, or have done some web development, chances are you know JavaScript. But do you know it enough to write professional code? Udacity’s JavaScript Design Patterns will not only allow you to write professional JavaScript code for better web applications, but will also give a deeper understanding of the MVC (Model View Controller) design pattern, which will improve your coding in general.

See JavaScript Design Patterns to get started right now.

6. Automata Theory

The Automata theory is one hell of a thing. Though not as fun as Harvard’s CS50, Standford’s Automata Theory online course is the best thing you would find online on the subject.

The reason I’m including this here is because the topic is quite perplexing and extremely important. It forms the foundation for compilers and programming languages.

The only prerequisite for this is Discrete Mathematics. MIT’s Mathematics for Computer Science is a good online course on it, and is sure to help you.

7. The Nature of Code - Daniel Shiffman

Daniel Shiffman has written an amazing book, The Nature of Code which focuses on the programming strategies and techniques behind computer simulations of natural systems using the programming language Processing. It’s purpose is explained in these questions on the book's website, “How can we capture the unpredictable evolutionary and emergent properties of nature in software? How can understanding the mathematical principles behind our physical world help us to create digital worlds?”

You can read it online for free or buy it to support the author. You should also subscribe to Daniel Shiffman’s YouTube channel The Coding Train! for learning amazing things about computer programming and algorithms.

8. Khanacademy

Khanacademy is the best website for learning really any subject. Whether it’s Physics, Mathematics, Biology, or Economics, Khanacademy has got you covered. There’s also an amazing section on Computer Science which explains different topics like Programming, Algorithms, and Cryptography.

Recently, they have started a new series on Computer Animation by Pixar studios, called Pixar In A Box.

9. SitePoint

SitePoint, founded in 1999, is a website which publishes articles for web developers, designers, programmers, product creators and entrepreneurs alike. They also create premium online courses on different topics.

You can learn a lot by visiting SitePoint once in a while.

10. TutsPlus

Envato Tuts+ publishes articles and tutorials in a wide range of categories, from Design & Illustration to Business and Music/Video. They also provide online courses and eBooks.
